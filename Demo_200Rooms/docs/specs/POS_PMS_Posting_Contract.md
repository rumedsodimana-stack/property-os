The POS to PMS integration defines the authoritative and auditable flow of all financial and operational transactions between the Point of Sale system and the Property Management System. In this architecture, the POS is the system of record for order creation, itemization, pricing, discounts, and payment capture, while the PMS remains the system of record for guest folios, city ledger accounts, business dates, and final accounting. The integration is designed to ensure zero revenue leakage, prevent duplicate postings, and remain fully compatible with night audit operations.

All financial communication between the POS and PMS is event-driven and immutable. Each completed POS transaction generates a discrete posting event that represents a final, auditable financial intent. Once generated, these events cannot be altered or deleted and must always be reversed through explicit counter-events rather than modification. This guarantees full traceability and audit compliance. The PMS must treat each incoming event idempotently, meaning that the same event cannot be processed more than once under any circumstance, even if re-sent due to network or system failures.

Before a room charge can be posted, the POS must validate that the room exists, the room is currently occupied or eligible for posting, the guest name matches the active folio, the folio is open, and the outlet is authorized to post charges. If any of these validations fail, the POS must prevent the transaction from proceeding and clearly inform the user. Under no circumstances should an invalid room charge be silently queued or posted.

When an order is settled as a room charge, the POS finalizes the order and submits a posting event to the PMS integration layer. The PMS validates the business date, revenue codes, tax configuration, and posting permissions before applying the charge to the guest folio. Once posted, the PMS returns a unique reference identifier that is permanently stored by the POS and linked to the original transaction. This reference forms the backbone of reconciliation, reversals, and audits.

Payment postings follow similar rules. When the POS collects payment on behalf of the PMS, such as advance settlements or combined bill scenarios, it sends a payment posting event that references the original order and payment method. The PMS retains full authority over financial settlement, and the POS is not permitted to modify or adjust payments after posting. Any correction must be handled through an explicit reversal event referencing the original payment.

Voids and reversals are strictly controlled. Transactions voided before posting require no PMS interaction. Once a transaction has been posted, it can never be deleted or edited. Instead, the POS must issue a reversal event that creates an offsetting negative charge or payment in the PMS. All reversals require appropriate permissions, mandatory reason capture, and full audit logging. This ensures historical accuracy and regulatory compliance.

The integration is fully night-audit aware. The POS operates on an outlet business date, while the PMS operates on the property business date. If the PMS night audit is in progress or closed, the POS must queue posting events until posting is permitted. When posted, the original business date is preserved, ensuring revenue is attributed to the correct accounting period. Back-dated postings are restricted and allowed only through controlled administrative processes.

Failure handling is designed to be resilient and safe. If the PMS is unavailable, the POS queues all posting events locally and retries automatically once connectivity is restored. Partial postings are not allowed; events must either be fully accepted or fully rejected. Duplicate events are ignored by the PMS, and configuration mismatches such as tax or revenue code errors result in hard failures that require correction before retrying.

Daily reconciliation is mandatory. POS end-of-day totals must reconcile exactly with PMS revenue totals by outlet, revenue code, and payment type. Any variance is automatically flagged for investigation. Every transaction maintains a complete audit trail, including user, device, timestamp, outlet, POS reference, and PMS reference. No financial record can be removed from the system.

Security is enforced through encrypted communication, authenticated integration credentials, and strict role-based access. Integration events are signed and verified to prevent tampering. Auditors are granted read-only access to replay events and verify postings without the ability to alter data.

This POS to PMS posting contract ensures financial accuracy, operational reliability, audit readiness, and enterprise-grade control across all food and beverage, retail, and ancillary outlets within the hotel ecosystem.
